What blocks student achievement in East Boston?

East Boston High School has a lower graduation rate than many other schools in Boston and Massachusetts. This project aims to find the story behind the data.

By Chenrui Ye


Do factors affect student performance?

Two of the main factors contributing to low student achievement are poverty and being a non-native speaker of English. We measure non-native speakers by counting the number of English Language Learners, or ELL. These charts show the relation between poverty and ELL enrollment and low rates of high school graduation.


How about East Boston compared with Boston and Massachusetts?

According to the data from the East Boston High, which represents the East Boston greatly, that area has higher English Language Learner rate and more low income families. These factor lead to the lower high school graduation rate and lower college attendance.
